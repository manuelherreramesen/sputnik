Play Safe

Enjoy The Game, Play It Safe

Playing at BlingCity is an entertaining and exciting experience. We want all our Citizens to enjoy the games yet play it safe. We recommend that you keep the following guidelines in mind when playing:

  • Play for Fun - don't think of gambling as investing your money or a way of coping with emotional or physical stress;
  • Before you start set limits that are healthy for you and your personal economy,
    • Monitor your limit of wagers and losses
    • Limit your sessions
  • Stop playing when you reach your limits, regardless of whether you are winning or losing. Edit your Play Safe Profile to help you manage your activity and stay on top of your game. For more info see Create your Play Safe Profile below;
  • Quit while you're ahead - enjoy your winnings, Bling Avenue
  • Accept losses as a part of the game. Don't try and recoup your losses; chances are you’ll end up losing even more;
  • The key is to stay on top of your game - to play on the safe side!

Create Your Play Safe Profile

Healthy gambling is about self-control. To this end we have created a Play Safe Profile which automatically monitors your limits. Edit your setting in the column to the right. Play Safe Profile is an easy way to monitor:

  • how long your session lasts.
  • your loss limit within a day, week or month.
  • your bet limit within a day, week or month.

After setting your individual limits, this is what happens if you reach them:

  • Session time - The play session will automatically close down and you will be logged of the site. You will have to go through the log in process again.
  • Loss limit - Your game will close down and you will automatically be blocked from playing games and making deposits or withdrawals for your specified period of time.
  • Bet limit - Your game will close down and you will automatically be blocked from playing games and making deposits or withdrawals for your specified period of time.

You may edit your Play Safe settings at any moment. Decreasing your limits (making them harder on yourself) will have immediate effect. Increasing or removing your limits (making them easier on yourself) requires a 7 days cool-off period before taking effect. You may also edit your Play Safe Profile to take a break from playing at BlingCity for a specified period of time. If you feel that you would like to permanently exclude yourself from the site please contact our support team for assistance (link to support chat). Please note that such request will not be reversible.

Recognise A Problem

It is not easy to identify unhealthy or problematic gambling behavior, but it helps if you know what to look for. Below are some signs that may indicate unhealthy or problematic gambling behavior:

  • Mood changes - Irritable, stressed, depressed and/or distant.
  • Change in behavior - Gambling affecting relationships with family and friends. Absence from work, school and/or withdrawal from social activities.
  • More time consuming - Increased amount of time spent gambling or on a computer, lack of apparent interests and/ or leisure pursuits.
  • Change in personal economy - Uncontrolled spending and/or noteworthy change in personal economy, lying about or concealing gambling activity, borrowing money and/or struggling to pay bills.

If you are worried that someone you know may have lost control over their gambling we strongly recommend you to turn to professional help and assistance. If you have any questions or concerns regarding your own gambling activities we recommend that you take a look at the Self Test below.

Self Test

When conducting the test below, consider your behavior the latest 12 months. Answer each question with one of the following options:

Never = 0
Sometimes = 1
most of the time = 2
almost always = 3

During the last 12 months...

  1. Have you bet more than you could really afford to lose?
  2. Have you needed to gamble with larger amounts of money to get the same feeling of excitement?
  3. When you gambled, did you go back another day to try to win back the money you lost?
  4. Have you borrowed money or sold anything to get money to gamble?
  5. Have you felt that you might have a problem with gambling?
  6. Has gambling caused you any health problems, including stress or anxiety?
  7. Have people criticized your betting or told you that you had a gambling problem, regardless of whether or not you thought it was true?
  8. Has your gambling caused any financial problems for you or your household?
  9. Have you felt guilty about the way you gamble or what happens when you gamble?

* PGSI (PROBLEM GAMBLING SEVERITY INDEX)

Scoring Instructions for the PGSI

Total your score. The higher your score the greater the risk that your gambling is a problem.Score the following for each response:

Never = 0
Sometimes = 1
Most of the time = 2
Almost always = 3

Scores for the nine items are summed, and the results are interpreted as follows:

0 Non-problem gambling.
1 - 2 Low level of problems with few or no identified negative consequences.
3 - 7 Moderate level of problems leading to some negative consequences.
8 or more Problem gambling with negative consequences and a possible loss of control.

If your score indicates that you have a problem with your gambling, we strongly advice you seek professional help. More information about PGSI: www.problemgambling.ca/Pages/Home.aspx
